Lesson 9 — The <body> Section Basics

What is the <body>?

The <body> element is where all the visible content of a webpage goes. If you can see it in the browser window — text, images, links, lists, tables, forms — it lives inside the <body>.

Metaphor: Think of an HTML page as a house. The <head> is the blueprint (title, metadata, instructions). The <body> is the rooms, furniture, and everything you interact with.

What Goes Inside?

  • Headings: <h1>–<h6> structure your content.
  • Paragraphs: <p> hold blocks of text.
  • Images: <img> display pictures (always use alt text for accessibility).
  • Links: <a> connect to other pages or sites.
  • Lists: <ul> (bullets), <ol> (numbers), and <li> (items).
  • Rules & breaks: <hr> adds a line, <br> adds a line break.
  • Other elements: tables, forms, videos, audio, and more.

Example

<!DOCTYPE html>
<html lang="en">
  <head>
    <meta charset="UTF-8">
    <title>Body Example</title>
  </head>
  <body>
    <h1>Welcome to My Page</h1>
    <p>This is inside the body.</p>
    <img src="dog.jpg" alt="A happy dog">
    <hr>
    <p>Thanks for visiting!</p>
  </body>
</html>

Explanation: The <h1> heading, <p> paragraphs, and <img> image all appear inside the <body>. The <hr> visually separates content.

Common Mistakes

  • Putting <title> or <meta> inside <body> (they belong in <head>).
  • Forgetting to close tags, like <p>Hello without </p>.
  • Using multiple <body> tags — only one is allowed per page.

Quick Quiz

  1. What is the purpose of the <body> element?
  2. Which of these belongs in the <head>, not the <body>: <title>, <h1>, <p>?
  3. True/False: You can have two <body> elements in the same HTML page.
Answers
  • The <body> holds all the visible content of a webpage.
  • <title> belongs in <head>.
  • False — only one <body> per page.

Mini Project — Personal Homepage

Create a simple homepage inside the <body> tag with the following:

  • An <h1> with your name.
  • A <p> introducing yourself.
  • An <img> of something meaningful to you (with alt text).
  • A <ul> list of your 3 favorite hobbies.
  • An <a> link to your favorite website.

Save it as index.html and open it in your browser to view your page!

Did You Know?

HTML was invented by Tim Berners-Lee in 1991 at CERN. The very first webpage used a <body> section with simple text and links. That page still exists online as a piece of history.

FAQ

Q: Do I need special software to write HTML?
A: No — you can use any text editor and a browser.

Q: Can I leave out the <body> tag?
A: Browsers may try to fix it, but you should always include it for proper structure.

Q: Can I style the <body>?
A: Yes! CSS often targets the <body> to set fonts, colors, or backgrounds.
